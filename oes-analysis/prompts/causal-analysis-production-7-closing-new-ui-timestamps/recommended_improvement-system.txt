## Edge Case Integration Examples

### Example 1: FEE-HELP Repayment Specifics Edge Case - Natural Style

**Opportunity Context:** Student asks about exact FEE-HELP repayment thresholds and amounts

**Recommendation with Edge Case Integration:**
"This opportunity involves the FEE-HELP Repayment Specifics edge case. The consultant said 'When you earn over $45,000, you'll pay back about 1% of your income' which provides specific repayment figures that may be inaccurate. Instead, the consultant could say: 'That's a great question about FEE-HELP repayments. The system is designed so you only begin making repayments once your income reaches a threshold set by the government. The ATO website has a repayment calculator where you can enter different income scenarios to see exactly how the repayments would work. Would you like me to send you a link to that calculator after our call?' This approach demonstrates expertise by avoiding specific threshold figures that may change while directing the student to an authoritative source. It incorporates Information Clarification by addressing the concern directly and establishing credibility. For students more concerned about immediate payments, this could be modified to emphasize that there are no upfront payments required, allowing them to focus on studies while deferring repayments until their income reaches the threshold. Students with clear, accurate information about financial obligations are better equipped to make informed decisions and less likely to withdraw due to financial misunderstandings later in their educational journey."

### Example 2: Waitlist Situations Edge Case - Natural Style

**Recognition with Edge Case Integration:**
"This opportunity demonstrates excellent handling of the Waitlist Situations edge case. When the student inquired about the July intake, the consultant skillfully said: 'I'll add you to our waitlist for the July intake. While I can't provide a specific timeline for when we might have confirmation about a spot, we'll contact you as soon as we have more information. In the meantime, would it be helpful to discuss what documentation you could prepare so you're ready to move quickly when a spot becomes available?' This response masterfully balances appropriate vagueness about timelines with maintaining engagement through preparatory activities. The consultant sets appropriate expectations without creating false hopes while still advancing the enrollment process through document preparation as a productive next step. This approach creates incremental commitment through preparation activities that benefit the student regardless of the final outcome. Students who complete preparatory steps while on waitlists tend to be more ready to enroll when spots become available, and their enrollment process typically proceeds more smoothly due to advance preparation."

## Error Mode Detection

Ensure your analysis avoids these common error modes:

1. **Overly Structured Analysis**:
   - NEVER use numbered lists for your main analysis
   - NEVER use bullet points to break down components, techniques, or principles
   - NEVER structure your analysis with explicit sections and headers
   - NEVER use formulaic structures that repeat across analyses
   - AVOID rigid formats that make recommendations feel mechanical

2. **Generic Recommendations**:
   - Avoid vague, general advice lacking specific implementation examples
   - Do not provide broad principles without concrete language examples
   - Ensure all recommendations include verbatim language examples
   - Avoid edge case generalizations without specific handling guidance

3. **Non-Level 5 Recommendations**:
   - Avoid basic or intermediate-level advice
   - Do not suggest implementations that lack advanced techniques
   - Ensure every recommendation demonstrates mastery of pattern components
   - Avoid simplistic approaches to complex edge cases

4. **Missing Contextual Adaptation**:
   - Do not provide rigid recommendations without situational variations
   - Avoid one-size-fits-all approaches
   - Ensure recommendations include guidance for different contexts
   - Provide adaptations for edge case variations when applicable

5. **Boundary Violations**:
   - Administrative vs Strategic Planning
   - Career Guidance Boundaries
   - Support Service Boundaries
   - Workload Planning Boundaries
   - Financial Planning Boundaries
   - Edge Case Handling Boundaries

6. **Missing Direct Quotes**:
   - Do not provide recommendations without quoting what the consultant actually said
   - Avoid generic improvement suggestions without specific language examples
   - Do not skip the critical comparison between actual and improved approaches
   - Always include direct quotes when analyzing edge case handling

7. **Ignoring Score-Based Approach**:
   - Do not provide detailed improvement recommendations for scores ≥ 4
   - Do not simply praise approaches with scores ≤ 3 without specific improvements
   - Do not fail to reference the relevant justifications for low-scoring opportunities
   - Always check scores before determining recommendation vs. recognition approach

8. **Vague Impact Statements**:
   - Avoid generic statements about "improving outcomes"
   - Do not use broad generalizations about enrollment impacts
   - Ensure impact statements reference specific enrollment journey milestones
   - Connect edge case handling to specific enrollment outcomes

9. **Edge Case Handling Errors**:
   - Do not ignore applicable edge cases
   - Avoid contradicting edge case handling guidance
   - Do not apply edge case handling when not applicable
   - Ensure edge case identification is accurate and specific

If you encounter patterns that would lead to these boundary issues, recommend referring students to appropriate support services instead.

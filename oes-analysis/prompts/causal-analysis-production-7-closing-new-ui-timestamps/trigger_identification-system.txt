  * Understand why it does NOT qualify as a valid opportunity

  * Create a list of in-scope situations that definitely qualify
  * Create a list of out-of-scope situations that might seem relevant but aren't
  * Note any specific constraints mentioned in the definition

  * Note what student needs or concerns should trigger pattern recognition
  * Understand how to differentiate this pattern from similar patterns

### Step 4: Application Criteria Development
- Develop specific criteria for identifying valid opportunities in the transcript:
  * Create a checklist of student signals that indicate a potential opportunity
  * Establish criteria for determining high vs. medium vs. low impact opportunities
  * Develop rules for applying the scoring rubric consistently
  * Create validation tests to confirm an identified opportunity is truly in-scope
  * Note specific considerations from the definition that must be evaluated
  * Prepare for common edge cases based on the examples provided

### Step 5: Methodical Transcript Review
- Examine the transcript systematically using your developed criteria:
  * Scan for student signals that match your opportunity checklist
  * For each potential opportunity, verify it against your scope boundaries
  * Apply your validation tests to confirm it's truly a pattern match
  * Check the pre-annotated opportunities in the transcript for alignment
  * Verify against the llm_analysis_summary to confirm opportunity identification

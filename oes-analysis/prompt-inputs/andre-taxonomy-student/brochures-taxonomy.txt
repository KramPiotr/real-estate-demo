# TAXONOMY ARTIFACT 19: BROCHURES ACQUISITION CHANNEL BEHAVIORS
This taxonomy examines behavioral patterns specific to prospects who accessed detailed marketing materials such as brochures, course guides, or comprehensive program information, indicating thorough research approaches and systematic decision-making processes.

## 19.A: Content Depth and Comprehensiveness

### 19.A.i: Detailed Information Seeking Behavior
**Description:** Student actively seeks comprehensive, in-depth information about programs beyond what is readily available in basic marketing materials, demonstrating thorough research methodology.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for requests for detailed program information, course outlines, or comprehensive materials that go beyond surface-level descriptions.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I don't know if you have more of an information pack on the diploma, anything that's more in-depth than what it goes into?"
- Student: "She wants to look at what those first core subjects are, what constitutes the individual units would be really useful"
- Student: "Can you email me all the details that I need to know, rather than just looking at the website?"

### 19.A.ii: Supplementary Material Requests
**Description:** Student requests additional supporting materials, guides, or documentation to supplement their decision-making process, indicating systematic evaluation approach.

**Identification Guidance:** Note requests for course guides, subject selection information, or additional detailed materials beyond initial brochures.

**Examples:**
- Student: "Can you do me a favor? You're going to send me those two course guides where those subject selections were listed"
- Student: "It was just so I knew what subjects to use for the final course"
- Student: "I need to have a look at that. Give me 24, 48 hours to have a look at it"

### 19.A.iii: Course Guide Dependency and Usage
**Description:** Student demonstrates reliance on course guides and detailed program materials for understanding pathways, requirements, and program components.

**Identification Guidance:** Look for references to re-reading course guides, using them for pathway understanding, or positive feedback about guide clarity.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I went back and had a re-read on the course guide, just to remind myself of the pathway and the essential components"
- Student: "I think it's pretty straightforward, which is good"
- Student: "I've just downloaded the course outline. I need to have a look at that"

## 19.B: Information Format Preferences

### 19.B.i: Written Material Preference Indicators
**Description:** Student expresses preference for written, documented information that they can review at their own pace rather than verbal communication alone.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for requests to receive information in written format, preferences for email over phone, or desire for materials they can reference later.

**Examples:**
- Student: "It's okay if I could get most of the replies in writing on email, because I just don't have anywhere to write them down"
- Student: "Can you please email me these details so I can discuss with my partner and we can come to a conclusion"
- Student: "I'll send you the course information via email" / "Sure, sounds good"

### 19.B.ii: Downloadable Resource Utilization
**Description:** Student actively downloads and utilizes available digital resources such as brochures, course outlines, and program guides for their research process.

**Identification Guidance:** Note mentions of downloading materials, having downloaded brochures, or actively using downloaded resources in their inquiry.

**Examples:**
- Student: "Awesome thank you very much I've got the I've downloaded the brochure"
- Student: "I've just downloaded the course outline"
- Student: "I've downloaded the brochure yeah I'm just scrolling through now having a look at what's available"

### 19.B.iii: Consolidated Information Requests
**Description:** Student seeks to receive all relevant information in a single, comprehensive format rather than gathering information piecemeal from multiple sources.

**Identification Guidance:** Look for requests to consolidate information, preference for comprehensive packages, or desire to have all details in one place for comparison.

**Examples:**
- Student: "Can you email me all the details that I need to know, so I can compare that against the current places I'm looking at?"
- Student: "I'll send you the course information via email"
- Student: "Can you please email me these details so I can discuss with my partner"

## 19.C: Engagement and Response Patterns

### 19.C.i: Initial Excitement and Interest Indicators
**Description:** Student demonstrates positive emotional response and enthusiasm upon receiving or accessing detailed program materials, suggesting effective marketing material design.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for expressions of excitement, positive feedback about materials received, or enthusiastic responses to information accessed.

**Examples:**
- Student: "Awesome thank you very much I've got the I've downloaded the brochure"
- Student: "Oh my God, amazing... Your email's popped"
- Student: "That sounds like a fantastic way to study while working"

### 19.C.ii: Review and Evaluation Behaviors
**Description:** Student takes time to thoroughly review materials before making decisions, demonstrating methodical evaluation approach typical of brochure users.

**Identification Guidance:** Note mentions of taking time to review materials, re-reading information, or systematic evaluation processes.

**Examples:**
- Student: "Give me 24, 48 hours to have a look at it"
- Student: "I went back and had a re-read on the course guide"
- Student: "I'm just scrolling through now having a look at what's available"

### 19.C.iii: Information Sharing and Discussion Preparation
**Description:** Student indicates intention to share received materials with others for discussion or consultation, suggesting collaborative decision-making process.

**Identification Guidance:** Look for mentions of discussing information with partners, family, or advisors, or requests for materials suitable for sharing.

**Examples:**
- Student: "Can you please email me these details so I can discuss with my partner and we can come to a conclusion"
- Student: "She just wants to have more information to compare"
- Student: "So I can compare that against the current places I'm looking at"

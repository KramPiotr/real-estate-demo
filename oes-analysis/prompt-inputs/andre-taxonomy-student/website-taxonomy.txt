# TAXONOMY ARTIFACT 17: WEBSITE ACQUISITION CHANNEL BEHAVIORS
This taxonomy examines behavioral patterns and issues specific to prospects who discovered the institution through the website, including navigation difficulties, information gaps, and technical barriers that may impact conversion.

## 17.A: Information Access & Navigation Challenges

### 17.A.i: Content Gaps and Clarity Problems
**Description:** Student experiences difficulty finding comprehensive or clear information on the website, indicating potential content gaps or presentation issues that hinder decision-making.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for expressions of confusion about program details, difficulty understanding website content, or frustration with unclear information presentation.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I looked on the website and there's not a huge amount of information about the course"
- Student: "The website was a little bit confusing with the dates for non-online programs"
- Student: "I do human-centered design but it probably wasn't as clear as it could have been on the website"

### 17.A.ii: Navigation and Discoverability Challenges
**Description:** Student struggles to navigate the website effectively or locate specific information, suggesting issues with site architecture or user experience design.

**Identification Guidance:** Note mentions of difficulty finding specific pages, getting lost in the interface, or being unable to locate expected information despite searching.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I couldn't find my application online, I wasn't sure which website was the correct one"
- Student: "There's so many pathways within the user interface that you can kind of become bamboozled by it all"
- Student: "I was getting so much unrelated information. It was hard, but I think this is the right place to be"

### 17.A.iii: Search and Information Retrieval Difficulties
**Description:** Student has problems using website search functionality or retrieving specific information they need for their decision-making process.

**Identification Guidance:** Look for mentions of unsuccessful searches, difficulty finding course outlines, pricing information, or specific program details that should be readily available.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I tried looking up scholarship information and I couldn't really find much"
- Student: "What I did not see last time is just all the pricing information for the subjects"
- Student: "I was just looking for subject outlines but I'm getting a lot of spam mail, download browser back-to-back"

## 17.B: Technical Functionality Problems

### 17.B.i: Login and Authentication Issues
**Description:** Student encounters technical difficulties with website login processes, account access, or password management that prevent them from accessing necessary resources.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for mentions of being locked out, password reset problems, or inability to access student accounts or application portals.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I've been locked out, so I just have to redo that and then I'll be able to forward that in"
- Student: "I have logged in. It's saying that my ID is not working"
- Student: "I tried to do the password reset, now I can't log into my profile"

### 17.B.ii: Button and Link Functionality Failures
**Description:** Student experiences non-functional website elements such as broken buttons, links, or interactive features that prevent task completion.

**Identification Guidance:** Note reports of buttons not working, links leading nowhere, or interactive elements failing to respond as expected.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I pressed accept, but nothing's happened. Does it take me to another page?"
- Student: "The link is not private and I cannot open it"
- Student: "When I tried to book it wouldn't come up with any online options"

### 17.B.iii: Device Compatibility and Access Problems
**Description:** Student encounters issues accessing or using the website on specific devices or due to technical limitations, affecting their ability to complete tasks.

**Identification Guidance:** Look for mentions of mobile device issues, browser compatibility problems, or technical requirements that create barriers to website usage.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I'm just trying to find an accept thing, it's not going through on my phone for some reason"
- Student: "My internet's a bit slow today, so I'm still trying to open it"
- Student: "Maybe I'm trying to open on my phone, once I get to my laptop, I'll try and open it again"

## 17.C: Content Accuracy and Completeness

### 17.C.i: Inaccurate or Conflicting Information
**Description:** Student discovers discrepancies between website information and actual program details, or finds conflicting information across different website sections.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for mentions of incorrect information found on the website, confusion due to conflicting details, or clarification requests about website content accuracy.

**Examples:**
- Student: "On the website it says COVID support is available for that course" [when it's not actually available]
- Student: "Because on the website it said six weeks I was like huh?" [when duration was different]
- Student: "It's telling me it's not available in Australia, which is pretty weird because the website says it is available"

### 17.C.ii: Missing Critical Information
**Description:** Student cannot find essential information on the website that they need to make enrollment decisions, such as prerequisites, costs, or application requirements.

**Identification Guidance:** Note requests for information that should be available on the website but appears to be missing or insufficiently detailed.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I couldn't find the exact entry requirements, there isn't any calculator or specific outline"
- Student: "What else is involved in the application because I can't seem to find it on the internet"
- Student: "The site doesn't really show that much, I think that's why I applied for it"

### 17.C.iii: Program-Specific Detail Deficiencies
**Description:** Student seeks more detailed information about specific programs, specializations, or course components that is not adequately covered on the website.

**Identification Guidance:** Look for requests for additional program details, course outlines, or specialization information that goes beyond what's currently available on the website.

**Examples:**
- Student: "She wants to look at what those first core subjects are, what constitutes the individual units"
- Student: "I don't know if you have more of an information pack on the diploma, anything that's more in-depth"
- Student: "Can you email me all the details rather than just looking at the website and all that sort of stuff?"

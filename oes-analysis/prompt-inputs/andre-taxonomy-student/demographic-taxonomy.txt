# TAXONOMY ARTIFACT 6: DEMOGRAPHIC & LIFE CONTEXT INDICATORS
This taxonomy examines student life circumstances and demographic factors that emerge through sales call discussions and may predict successful program completion.

## 6.A: Professional Context & Career Readiness

### 6.A.i: Career Transition Timing Optimization
**Description:** Student demonstrates they are at an optimal career transition point where additional education will have immediate professional impact.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for mentions of recent promotions, job changes, new responsibilities, or explicit statements about career advancement opportunities that education would support.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I just got promoted to team leader and my manager said I should get formal qualifications"
- Student: "My company is expanding and they want someone with project management credentials for the new role"
- Student: "I'm switching from clinical work to management and need the business skills"

### 6.A.ii: Employer Educational Support Indicators
**Description:** Student mentions employer encouragement, financial support, or professional development programs that indicate institutional backing for their education.

**Identification Guidance:** Note references to company tuition assistance, manager encouragement, professional development budgets, or workplace learning cultures.

**Examples:**
- Student: "My employer covers 50% of tuition for job-related courses"
- Student: "My boss actually recommended this program and said I could adjust my hours during busy periods"
- Student: "Our company has a professional development fund I can access"

### 6.A.iii: Industry Growth Sector Alignment
**Description:** Student works in expanding industries or roles where upskilling is particularly valued and career advancement opportunities are abundant.

**Identification Guidance:** Identify mentions of growing fields like healthcare, technology, renewable energy, or roles in high-demand areas where additional qualifications create clear advantages.

**Examples:**
- Student: "Healthcare administration is really growing and they need people with business qualifications"
- Student: "The tech industry values continuous learning and my company encourages formal education"
- Student: "With all the infrastructure projects happening, project management skills are in high demand"

## 6.B: Life Stage & Stability Factors

### 6.B.i: Optimal Life Stage Positioning
**Description:** Student demonstrates they are at a life stage with relatively stable circumstances that support sustained educational commitment.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for indicators of stability such as established routines, children of appropriate ages, settled housing situations, or completion of major life transitions.

**Examples:**
- Student: "The kids are finally in high school so I have more time in the evenings"
- Student: "We just bought our house last year and are settled, so this feels like good timing"
- Student: "I've been in this job for three years now and feel ready to take on something new"

### 6.B.ii: Family Configuration Advantages
**Description:** Student describes family circumstances that will support rather than hinder their educational commitment.

**Identification Guidance:** Note mentions of supportive partners, independent children, available childcare, or family members who encourage education.

**Examples:**
- Student: "My partner is really supportive and said they'll handle more of the household stuff while I'm studying"
- Student: "My teenagers are pretty independent now, so I don't need to be hands-on with homework anymore"
- Student: "My parents live nearby and can help with the kids when I need to focus on assignments"

### 6.B.iii: Geographic & Infrastructure Advantages
**Description:** Student mentions location factors that support online education success, such as reliable infrastructure or strategic positioning relative to traditional alternatives.

**Identification Guidance:** Look for references to reliable internet, dedicated study spaces, rural/remote locations where online is optimal, or urban areas with good infrastructure.

**Examples:**
- Student: "Living in a rural area, online is really my only option for this type of program"
- Student: "I have excellent internet here and a dedicated home office space"
- Student: "The closest university is two hours away, so online makes much more sense"

## 6.C: Financial Context & Investment Readiness

### 6.C.i: Financial Planning Evidence
**Description:** Student demonstrates thoughtful financial planning and realistic understanding of educational investment requirements.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for mentions of budgeting, savings, understanding of total costs, research into payment options, or strategic timing of educational investment.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I've been saving for this for two years and have the funds set aside"
- Student: "I've looked into the HECS options and understand how the repayments work"
- Student: "I want to start this year because I'll get a tax deduction for work-related study"

### 6.C.ii: Income Stability & Growth Potential
**Description:** Student indicates stable income with potential for growth that education would support, suggesting ability to sustain financial commitments.

**Identification Guidance:** Note references to steady employment, salary progression potential, dual incomes, or financial security that supports educational investment.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I've been in this role for five years with regular pay increases"
- Student: "Between my partner and I, we're in a good financial position to take this on"
- Student: "The qualification should help me move to the next salary band at work"

### 6.C.iii: Investment Mindset Indicators
**Description:** Student frames education as a strategic investment rather than just a cost, indicating sophisticated financial thinking.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for language that connects education costs to future returns, career advancement, or long-term financial benefits.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I see this as an investment in my future earning potential"
- Student: "The course fee will pay for itself within two years if I get the promotion"
- Student: "My accountant said education expenses are tax-deductible for my situation"

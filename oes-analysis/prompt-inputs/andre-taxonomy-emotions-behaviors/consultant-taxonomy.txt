# CONSULTANT EMOTIONS AND BEHAVIORS TAXONOMY

This taxonomy identifies key emotional and behavioral patterns exhibited by consultants in educational sales calls.

---

## CONSULTANT EMOTIONS

### C.E.1: Empathy Expression
**Speaker**: Consultant
**Feature Code**: C.E.1
**Definition**: Consultant acknowledges and validates student's feelings, concerns, or challenges.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Acknowledges student emotions: "I understand that's concerning"
- Validates concerns rather than dismissing them
- Shows understanding of student situation

**Example**: "That's a valid concern - many students feel that way initially."

---

### C.E.2: Enthusiasm/Energy
**Speaker**: Consultant
**Feature Code**: C.E.2
**Definition**: Consultant demonstrates positive energy and excitement about the program.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Upbeat tone and language
- Excitement about program benefits
- Enthusiastic responses to student interest

**Example**: "That's fantastic that you're interested in this area!" or "You're going to love this unit."

---

## CONSULTANT BEHAVIORS

### C.B.1: Artificial Urgency Creation
**Speaker**: Consultant
**Feature Code**: C.B.1
**Definition**: Consultant creates false time pressure or uses scarcity tactics to push for immediate enrollment.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Claims about limited spots: "Only a few spots left"
- Arbitrary deadlines: "I need your decision by tomorrow"
- Fear-based language: "I'd hate for you to miss out"

**Example**: "We only have a few spots left in this intake" (when not actually true).

---

### C.B.2: Objection Dismissal
**Speaker**: Consultant
**Feature Code**: C.B.2
**Definition**: Consultant quickly dismisses or minimizes legitimate student concerns.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Downplays concerns: "Oh, everyone feels that way"
- Changes subject when concerns raised
- Generic reassurance without addressing specific concern

---

### C.B.3: Excessive Promise-Making
**Speaker**: Consultant
**Feature Code**: C.B.3
**Definition**: Consultant makes unrealistic promises about outcomes or support that exceed what's deliverable.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Guarantees about results: "This degree virtually guarantees promotion"
- Unrealistic availability promises: "I'll personally be available whenever"
- Specific outcome promises without evidence

**Example**: "You'll definitely get a $20K salary increase after graduation."

---

### C.B.4: Comprehensive Needs Assessment
**Speaker**: Consultant
**Feature Code**: C.B.4
**Definition**: Consultant conducts thorough exploration of student needs, goals, and circumstances.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Multiple probing questions about student situation
- Open-ended questions: "Tell me about your career goals"
- Refers back to student's stated needs

**Example**: "Before I explain the program, can you tell me more about what you're hoping to achieve?"

---

### C.B.5: Realistic Expectation Setting
**Speaker**: Consultant
**Feature Code**: C.B.5
**Definition**: Consultant provides balanced, honest information about program challenges and benefits.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Acknowledges both positives and challenges
- Honest about workload demands
- Explains limitations as well as benefits

**Example**: "Many students do find the first unit challenging while adjusting to online study."

---

### C.B.6: Clear Process Guidance
**Speaker**: Consultant
**Feature Code**: C.B.6
**Definition**: Consultant provides clear, specific instructions for completing enrollment steps.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Step-by-step explanations
- Confirms understanding: "Does that make sense?"
- Anticipates common challenges

**Example**: "First, you'll receive an email with a link. Click that link to create your account, then..."

---

### C.B.7: Proactive Follow-Up Planning
**Speaker**: Consultant
**Feature Code**: C.B.7
**Definition**: Consultant establishes specific plans for follow-up communication.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Concrete follow-up plans: "I'll call you next Tuesday"
- Specific timing established
- Clear ownership of next steps

**Example**: "So you'll submit those documents by Friday, and I'll call Monday to confirm receipt."

# STUDENT EMOTIONS AND BEHAVIORS TAXONOMY

This taxonomy identifies key emotional and behavioral patterns exhibited by students in educational sales calls.

---

## STUDENT EMOTIONS

### S.E.1: Linguistic Uncertainty
**Speaker**: Student
**Feature Code**: S.E.1
**Definition**: Student uses verbal patterns indicating unresolved doubts or hesitation about enrollment decisions.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Frequent hedge words: "I guess," "maybe," "probably," "I might"
- Conditional language: "If I decide to enroll..." (not "When I enroll...")
- Repeated questions about same topics

**Example**: Student says "I guess that could work" or "Maybe I should think about it" when discussing enrollment.

---

### S.E.2: Online Study Anxiety
**Speaker**: Student
**Feature Code**: S.E.2
**Definition**: Student expresses specific concerns or surprise about the fully online learning format.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Direct concern about online learning effectiveness
- Surprise about lack of in-person components
- Questions about learning without face-to-face interaction

**Example**: "I was hoping there would be some face-to-face classes" or "I'm not sure I can learn effectively online."

---

### S.E.3: Overwhelm Indicators
**Speaker**: Student
**Feature Code**: S.E.3
**Definition**: Student shows signs of feeling overwhelmed by information, decisions, or perceived difficulty.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Explicit statements: "This is a lot to take in"
- Difficulty tracking conversation
- Repeated requests to repeat information

**Example**: "I don't know where to start" or showing scattered focus throughout the call.

---

### S.E.4: Enthusiasm Expression
**Speaker**: Student
**Feature Code**: S.E.4
**Definition**: Student expresses excitement about program benefits and career advancement opportunities.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Positive emotional expressions about learning
- Excitement about career advancement
- Spontaneous discussion of applying knowledge

**Example**: "I'm really excited to learn these skills" or "I can't wait to apply this in my job."

---

## STUDENT BEHAVIORS

### S.B.1: Low Question Engagement
**Speaker**: Student
**Feature Code**: S.B.1
**Definition**: Student asks few specific questions about the program, suggesting limited engagement.

**Observable Indicators**:
- No questions about program content
- Only superficial questions
- Doesn't seek clarification on important points

**Example**: Student responds passively without asking about requirements, structure, or content.

---

### S.B.2: Specific Commitment Statements
**Speaker**: Student
**Feature Code**: S.B.2
**Definition**: Student makes specific, actionable commitments about completing enrollment steps.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Specific timeframes: "I'll submit documents tonight"
- Immediate action plans: "I'll create my account right after this call"
- Clear ownership: "I will complete the form"

**Example**: "I'll upload my transcript this afternoon" (not "I'll try to get to that soon").

---

### S.B.3: Future Self-Projection
**Speaker**: Student
**Feature Code**: S.B.3
**Definition**: Student envisions themselves as a student/graduate in concrete, specific terms.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Discusses applying knowledge in current role
- Plans study schedule
- Talks about post-graduation career changes

**Example**: "I'm looking forward to applying these accounting principles at work" or "After I graduate, I'll apply for the senior position."

---

### S.B.4: Conflict Avoidance
**Speaker**: Student
**Feature Code**: S.B.4
**Definition**: Student demonstrates reluctance to express disagreement or concern directly.

**Observable Indicators**:
- Minimal passive responses: "sure," "okay" without enthusiasm
- Indirect concern expressions: "My friend had trouble..." (referring to own concerns)
- Vague deferrals: "I need to think about it" without specifying concerns

**Example**: Student gives polite agreement but doesn't demonstrate genuine commitment.

---

## USAGE NOTES

**For Analysis**:
- Evaluate each feature independently
- Only include features that are clearly present in the transcript
- Reference segment IDs where the feature appears
- Rate intensity and confidence for each present feature

**Feature Codes**:
- S.E.X = Student Emotion features
- S.B.X = Student Behavior features

<behavior_name>Confidence and Expertise</behavior_name>

<behavior_definition>
Demonstrating knowledge and assurance in providing information and answering questions. This involves being well-prepared with accurate information and communicating it clearly and authoritatively, instilling trust in the student and reinforcing the credibility of the institution.
</behavior_definition>

<behavior_essence>
Confidence and expertise are about projecting a strong, knowledgeable presence that reassures students and enhances their perception of the institution. The consultant demonstrates this by providing detailed, accurate information without hesitation, explaining complex concepts clearly, and showing a deep understanding of the programs and processes. This approach helps students feel that they are in capable hands and receiving reliable guidance.
</behavior_essence>

<behavior_examples>
* Providing detailed, accurate information about course structure, content, and career outcomes without hesitation.
* Confidently explaining complex concepts like credit transfers or admission requirements in a clear, understandable way.
* Using phrases like "Based on my experience with similar cases..." to demonstrate expertise.
* Quickly accessing and sharing relevant information about specific programs or policies.
* Offering insights into how the course content relates to industry trends or career opportunities.
* Demonstrating "Adequate analysis of student needs" by providing tailored information that precisely addresses the student's situation.
* Using authoritative language: "I can assure you that our program is designed to..." or "You can be confident that our support services..."
</behavior_examples>
</behavior_specific>

# TAXONOMY ARTIFACT 18: FORMS ACQUISITION CHANNEL BEHAVIORS
This taxonomy examines behavioral patterns and issues specific to prospects who initiated contact through completing online forms, including application difficulties, communication breakdowns, and process confusion that may impact enrollment completion.

## 18.A: Application Process Barriers

### 18.A.i: Process Clarity and Instruction Issues
**Description:** Student demonstrates confusion about application procedures, next steps, or requirements after submitting initial forms, indicating insufficient guidance or unclear instructions.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for uncertainty about what happens after form submission, confusion about required steps, or requests for clarification about the application process.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I would just like to put the application through, but it said I had to call first before moving forward with the application"
- Student: "When I submitted my application, I didn't get any email... I wanted to know what other things I need to send through"
- Student: "I just wasn't sure if I have to reply to that with my documentation or if there's a proper application portal"

### 18.A.ii: Technical Submission Difficulties
**Description:** Student encounters technical problems when attempting to submit forms or upload required documents, creating barriers to completing the application process.

**Identification Guidance:** Note mentions of upload failures, technical errors during submission, or inability to complete form-related tasks due to system issues.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I tried to upload a picture of my passport on the enrollment and it didn't really like when I tried to do that"
- Student: "So I just wanted to ask, have you got the files? My end is telling me error"
- Student: "I've been trying to change my username and password and complete my enrollment and questionnaire but I haven't had much success"

### 18.A.iii: Document Requirements Confusion
**Description:** Student expresses uncertainty about which documents are needed, acceptable formats, or certification requirements for their application submission.

**Identification Guidance:** Look for questions about specific document requirements, confusion about document formats, or uncertainty about certification needs.

**Examples:**
- Student: "Does a transcript need to be JP certified or can you just have a transcript?"
- Student: "I also have a question about that diploma... when I was filling out the application it was saying that was a requirement"
- Student: "What other things do I need to send through, because I didn't get that confirmation?"

## 18.B: Communication and Follow-up Problems

### 18.B.i: Missing Confirmation Communications
**Description:** Student did not receive expected confirmation emails, acknowledgments, or follow-up communications after submitting forms, leading to uncertainty about application status.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for mentions of missing confirmation emails, lack of acknowledgment after form submission, or uncertainty about whether the application was received.

**Examples:**
- Student: "When I submitted my application, I didn't get any email"
- Student: "I didn't receive like an email follow-up... I'm assuming it's like an automated system"
- Student: "I still haven't received an email to do the student stuff"

### 18.B.ii: Email Delivery and Response Issues
**Description:** Student experiences problems with email communications, including emails not being received, going to spam, or delayed responses from the institution.

**Identification Guidance:** Note mentions of emails not arriving, spam filter issues, or delays in receiving expected communications from the institution.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I tried to send you a quick email. Unfortunately, the email just bounced back"
- Student: "The spam filter on my email is continuously blocking the actual letter of offer"
- Student: "I haven't got a call and I just got an email saying that my offer expires tomorrow"

### 18.B.iii: Account Setup and Access Problems
**Description:** Student encounters difficulties setting up their student account, accessing login credentials, or receiving account setup instructions after form submission.

**Identification Guidance:** Look for mentions of account setup issues, missing login details, or problems accessing student portals after initial form completion.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I did speak to someone on Saturday... She said I will receive an email for my Monash online login"
- Student: "I was going to call back about my enrollment. I haven't received any further calls or emails to create an online account"
- Student: "I received instructions for setting up your Monash Online account but I just haven't gone around to setting it up yet"

## 18.C: User Experience and Interface Issues

### 18.C.i: Form Design and Usability Problems
**Description:** Student finds the online forms confusing, difficult to navigate, or poorly designed, impacting their ability to complete the submission process effectively.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for complaints about form complexity, navigation difficulties, or design issues that create barriers to completion.

**Examples:**
- Student: "Actually, the online form bit was pretty, could be, was a bit confusing"
- Student: "I try to get some units in, then it refreshes and crashes, and I can't even find units or what electives it will let me do"
- Student: "The office application website... should I just be putting through all the documents on there?"

### 18.C.ii: Authentication and Password Management
**Description:** Student experiences difficulties with password creation, reset processes, or authentication requirements that prevent them from progressing through the application process.

**Identification Guidance:** Note mentions of password problems, authentication failures, or difficulties with login credential management during the application process.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I've been trying to change my username and password and complete my enrollment"
- Student: "I got the link I need from the website, but I can't remember my password and I've been locked out"
- Student: "I tried to do the password reset, now I can't log into my profile"

### 18.C.iii: Multi-step Process Breakdown
**Description:** Student becomes confused or lost during multi-step application processes, unable to track progress or understand what stage they are in.

**Identification Guidance:** Look for expressions of confusion about application stages, uncertainty about progress, or difficulty understanding what steps remain to complete enrollment.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I haven't got a call and I just got an email saying that my offer expires tomorrow and I should enroll in units"
- Student: "I clicked the link to confirm enrollment and I got a student number... I thought that was enough"
- Student: "There is still a couple of steps left to enrollment... I can see you have applied"

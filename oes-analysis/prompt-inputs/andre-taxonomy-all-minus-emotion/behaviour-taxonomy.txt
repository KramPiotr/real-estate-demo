# TAXONOMY ARTIFACT 3: SALES CONSULTANT BEHAVIORS

This taxonomy examines sales consultant approaches that may influence student progression through enrollment gates.

## 3.A: Pressure Tactics & Misaligned Incentives

### 3.A.i: Artificial Urgency Creation
**Description**: Consultant creates false time pressure or uses scarcity tactics to push for immediate enrollment.

**Identification Guidance**: Look for statements suggesting limited spots, imminent deadlines, or other time pressures that may not be accurate or relevant.

**Examples**:
- Consultant: "We only have a few spots left in this intake"
- Consultant: "I'd hate for you to miss out if you wait too long to decide"
- Consultant creates arbitrary deadlines: "I need your decision by tomorrow"

### 3.A.ii: Objection Dismissal/Minimization
**Description**: Consultant quickly dismisses or minimizes legitimate student concerns rather than addressing them substantively.

**Identification Guidance**: Note instances where student concerns are downplayed, redirected, or dismissed without adequate exploration or resolution.

**Examples**:
- Student: "I'm worried about the time commitment" â†’ Consultant: "Oh, everyone feels that way at first, it's no big deal"
- Consultant changes the subject when student raises concerns
- Consultant provides generic reassurance without addressing the specific concern

### 3.A.iii: Excessive Promise-Making
**Description**: Consultant makes unrealistic promises about outcomes, support, or the student experience that exceed what the institution can deliver.

**Identification Guidance**: Identify specific promises about results, support levels, or experiences that appear exaggerated or unverifiable.

**Examples**:
- Consultant: "This degree virtually guarantees you'll get a promotion"
- Consultant: "I'll personally be available whenever you need help" (when this isn't their role)
- Consultant makes specific salary increase promises without evidence

## 3.B: Student-Centered Approaches

### 3.B.i: Comprehensive Needs Assessment
**Description**: Consultant conducts thorough exploration of student needs, goals, and circumstances before discussing program fit.

**Identification Guidance**: Look for open-ended questions about the student's situation, active listening behaviors, and tailoring of information to stated needs.

**Examples**:
- Consultant asks multiple probing questions before making recommendations
- Consultant: "Can you tell me more about your career goals so I can explain how this program might help?"
- Consultant refers back to student's stated needs when discussing program features

### 3.B.ii: Realistic Expectation Setting
**Description**: Consultant provides balanced, honest information about program challenges and benefits, particularly when addressing student concerns.

**Identification Guidance**: Note instances where the consultant acknowledges both positive and challenging aspects of the program, especially when directly asked.

**Examples**:
- Consultant: "Many students do find the first unit challenging while adjusting to online study"
- Consultant honestly discusses workload demands when asked
- Consultant explains both the benefits and limitations of support services

### 3.B.iii: Fit-Based Recommendation
**Description**: Consultant makes recommendations based on genuine program-student fit rather than pushing for enrollment regardless of appropriateness.

**Identification Guidance**: Look for instances where the consultant acknowledges potential misalignment or suggests alternatives when appropriate.

**Examples**:
- Consultant suggests a different program that better matches student goals
- Consultant recommends starting with fewer units based on student's situation
- Consultant is willing to suggest delaying enrollment if timing isn't right

## 3.C: Enrollment Process Facilitation

### 3.C.i: Step-by-Step Guidance Provision
**Description**: Consultant provides clear, specific instructions for completing the enrollment process, particularly regarding Hub navigation and account creation.

**Identification Guidance**: Note detailed explanations of enrollment steps, account creation procedures, and Hub navigation that leave little room for confusion.

**Examples**:
- Consultant explains exact steps: "First, you'll receive an email with a link. Click that link to create your account, then log in to the Hub where you'll see..."
- Consultant confirms understanding: "Does that make sense? Can I clarify anything about these steps?"
- Consultant anticipates common challenges: "Some people find that section confusing, so let me explain..."

### 3.C.ii: Document Requirement Clarity
**Description**: Consultant clearly explains all required documentation, submission methods, and deadlines.

**Identification Guidance**: Look for specific, comprehensive information about required documents and submission processes.

**Examples**:
- Consultant provides a complete list: "You'll need to submit your transcript, ID, and proof of residency"
- Consultant explains format requirements: "They'll accept scanned copies or clear photos of your documents"
- Consultant discusses deadlines: "To secure your spot in this intake, we need these documents by [specific date]"

### 3.C.iii: Proactive Follow-Up Planning
**Description**: Consultant establishes specific plans for follow-up communication to support the student through enrollment steps.

**Identification Guidance**: Note concrete plans for future contact, specific timing, and clear ownership of next steps.

**Examples**:
- Consultant: "I'll follow up with you next Tuesday to see how you're progressing with your application"
- Consultant establishes accountability: "So you'll submit those documents by Friday, and I'll call on Monday to confirm receipt"
- Consultant provides direct contact information: "If you have any questions before then, you can reach me at..."

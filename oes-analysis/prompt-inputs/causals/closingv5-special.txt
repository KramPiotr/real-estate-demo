# ENROLLMENT CLOSING SPECIAL ANALYSIS VERIFICATION FRAMEWORK

## DOCUMENT OVERVIEW AND USAGE GUIDE

This special file addresses two critical correction requirements for enrollment closing analysis:

1. **Teaching Period Confirmation Focus Correction**: Ensuring analysis focuses on commitment-seeking actions that follow calendar information, not on the calendar information itself. Analysis must emphasize how consultants transform date provision into enrollment commitment.

2. **Commitment Language Correction**: Ensuring all recommended language follows approved patterns, avoiding direct enrollment claims ("Let's secure your spot") in favor of commitment questions ("Were you looking to commence in July?") followed by document requests.

**Document Structure:**
- **Decision Trees**: Logical pathways to determine if analysis requires correction
- **Correction Instructions**: Step-by-step guidance immediately following each decision point
- **Before/After Examples**: Concrete demonstrations of proper corrections
- **Verification Checklists**: Final confirmation that all requirements are met

**How to Apply This Framework:**
1. Process each analysis section (opportunity, response, recommendation) through relevant decision trees
2. When correction is required, apply the instructions that directly follow the decision point
3. Preserve all evidence (quotes, segment numbers) while adjusting only the analytical focus
4. Verify final output against the checklist before proceeding

## TEACHING PERIOD CONFIRMATION ANALYSIS DECISION TREE

### Step 1: Analyze Focus Assessment
Does the analysis focus primarily on the consultant confirming the teaching period/intake start date?

- **If NO →** Reproduce analysis verbatim (no change required)
- **If YES →** Continue to Step 2

### Step 2: Post-Confirmation Analysis Check
Does the analysis also focus on what comes after the teaching period confirmation? Specifically:
- Does it analyze the assumptive or tentative close?
- Does it analyze the documentation request?
- Does it explicitly analyze the ABSENCE of these elements as a missed opportunity?

- **If YES →** Reproduce analysis verbatim (no change required)
- **If NO →** Apply the following correction:

**Correction Instructions:**
1. Maintain all evidence (quotes, segment numbers, factual content)
2. Reframe analysis to position calendar information as context rather than the primary opportunity
3. Add analysis of how effectively the consultant secured commitment (or failed to)
4. Use expressions like "uses the intake date as a springboard" instead of treating date confirmation as the primary action
5. Ensure analysis addresses the three-part sequence (information → commitment → documents)

**⚠️ CRITICAL EVIDENCE REQUIREMENT:**
When correcting analysis, you MUST ALWAYS include concrete quotes from the transcript that either:
- Show the presence of an assumptive/tentative close and documentation request, OR
- Explicitly identify the absence of these elements as a missed opportunity

Analysis without specific transcript quotes demonstrating the presence or absence of commitment-seeking language is UNACCEPTABLE and must be corrected to include such evidence.

**⚠️ TRANSCRIPT EXAMINATION REQUIREMENT:**
You MUST examine the transcript file to locate what happens AFTER the teaching period confirmation is stated. Specifically:
1. Identify where the consultant provides the calendar date/teaching period information
2. Look at the SUBSEQUENT dialogue in the transcript to determine if:
   - The consultant follows with an assumptive or tentative close question
   - The consultant requests documentation
   - The consultant fails to do either (missed opportunity)
3. Analyze the EXACT language used and quote it with segment numbers

### Assumptive vs. Tentative Close Framework

**Assumptive Close:**
- Used when the student has shown explicit signals of willingness to enroll or microsignals of commitment
- The consultant assumes their interest in enrolling for a specific start date
- Example: "Were you looking to commence in [start date]?" followed by "If you can send me through your [documents], I can assess that for the [month] intake"

**Tentative Close:**
- Used when the student has demonstrated some uncertainty
- Less direct approach that still seeks commitment while acknowledging potential hesitation
- Examples:
  - "Would [start date] work with your timeline?"
  - "Are you interested in joining the [month] intake?"

All analysis MUST correctly identify which approach (assumptive or tentative) was used or should have been used based on the student's signals of readiness.

## KEY LANGUAGE REQUIREMENTS

### Required Three-Part Sequence

ALL Teaching Period Confirmation analyses must address the complete three-part sequence:

1. **Calendar Information** (context only, never the focus)
   - Example: "The consultant provided information about the June 30th start date"

2. **Commitment Assessment** (critical focus)
   - Example: "The consultant followed with 'Were you looking to commence in July?'"

3. **Documentation Transition** (critical focus)
   - Example: "The consultant then requested specific documents: 'I'd need your transcript by next Friday'"

### Language Usage Rules

#### FORBIDDEN LANGUAGE (NEVER USE OR RECOMMEND):
- "Let's secure your spot for [month] now"
- "I can secure your place in the [month] intake"
- "Let's go ahead and confirm your enrollment for [month]"
- Any variation of direct enrollment confirmation language

#### APPROVED LANGUAGE (ALWAYS USE AND RECOMMEND):

**Assumptive Close** (for students showing clear readiness signals):
- "Were you looking to commence in [month]?"
- Followed immediately by: "Great, then I'd just need [specific documents] from you to proceed with the [month] intake"

**Tentative Close** (for students showing some uncertainty):
- "Would [start date] work with your timeline?"
- "Are you interested in joining the [month] intake?"
- Followed by: "If that timing works for you, we'd need [documents] to get the process started"

**⚠️ CRITICAL QUOTATION REQUIREMENT:**
When analyzing consultant responses or making recommendations:
1. ALWAYS include DIRECT QUOTES from the transcript showing the exact language used
2. For recommendations, provide CONCRETE EXAMPLES of the exact phrasing consultants should use
3. Generic language descriptions without specific quotes are UNACCEPTABLE
4. Analysis must clearly distinguish between assumptive and tentative closing approaches based on student readiness signals

**Contextual Selection Guide:**
- Use assumptive close language when student has shown explicit willingness to enroll or microsignals of commitment
- Use tentative close language when student has demonstrated some uncertainty or is still early in decision-making
- ALWAYS identify which approach is appropriate based on specific student signals from the transcript

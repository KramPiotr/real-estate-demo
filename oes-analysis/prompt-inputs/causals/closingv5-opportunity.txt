# Behaviour Name: 'Enrolment Closing'

# Enrollment Closing Opportunity Framework: Comprehensive Definition with Examples

## Core Definition

An Enrollment Closing Opportunity occurs when a student demonstrates readiness to move forward in the enrollment process through direct or indirect signals, and the call has progressed sufficiently through the discovery and value-building phases to warrant securing commitment for specific next steps.

These opportunities enable consultants to establish mutual accountability, confirm concrete action steps with explicit timelines, schedule specific follow-up interactions, and maintain enrollment momentum.

## âš ï¸ CRITICAL DISTINCTION: Calendar Date vs. Teaching Period Confirmation

When analyzing sales calls, it is **ABSOLUTELY CRITICAL** to understand that simply providing or confirming a calendar start date is **NEVER** a Teaching Period Confirmation opportunity on its own. This is one of the most common analysis errors and must be avoided.

### What IS NOT a Teaching Period Confirmation Opportunity (NEVER IDENTIFY THESE):
- A consultant merely stating when a course starts
- A consultant answering a student's question about intake dates
- Any exchange that only provides calendar information without securing commitment
- The initial mention of a start date without connecting it to enrollment action

**Example of INCORRECT Opportunity Analysis (From Screenshot):**

> This high-impact opportunity arose when the consultant mentioned, 'Okay, yeah perfect because we have our next intake commencing on the 30th of June...' (segment 60).

This analysis incorrectly focuses on the calendar date confirmation itself, which is merely informational and NOT an opportunity. The analysis mistakenly treats the statement of a start date as the opportunity.

### What IS a Teaching Period Confirmation Opportunity (ALWAYS FOCUS ON THESE):

The opportunity emerges ONLY when the consultant uses the calendar information as a springboard to:

1. **Secure an assumptive close**: "Were you looking to start in July then?"
2. **Request documentation for a specific intake**: "To secure your place in the July intake, I'll need [specific documents] by [date]."
3. **Establish mutual commitment**: Getting the student to verbally commit to a specific intake date
4. **Connect timeline to action steps**: "If you want to start on June 30th, we need to complete these steps by [date]."

**Example of CORRECT Opportunity Analysis:**

> This high-impact opportunity arose when, after informing the student about the June 30th start date (segment 60), the consultant immediately secured commitment with an assumptive close and documentation request: "So yeah, today or over the weekend, if you can respond to my email with the document" (segment 87). This aligns perfectly with Teaching Period Confirmation criteria by transforming calendar information into concrete enrollment action. The student's affirmative response "Yeah, yeah" indicates successful commitment establishment, making this a pivotal moment that anchors the student's interest to specific enrollment steps with clear timelines. By connecting the June 30th start date to immediate document submission, the consultant effectively converted informational discussion to enrollment commitment.

The analysis should ALWAYS focus on how the consultant used the calendar information to gain commitment, request documentation, or establish next steps - NEVER on the calendar information itself.

### Key Distinction Checklist:

## Comprehensive Scoring Rubric (1-5)

### 5 - Critical Opportunity (Highest Priority)
- **Definition**: A perfect opportunity for enrollment closing representing the highest possible impact on enrollment likelihood
- **Student Signals**: Student has explicitly expressed readiness to proceed or has asked direct questions about enrollment timing/process
- **Call Timing**: Occurs after thorough discovery, value-building, and objection handling
- **Context**: All necessary prerequisites for enrollment commitment have been addressed
- **Missed Impact**: Failure to capitalize would significantly reduce enrollment likelihood
- **Examples**: Student directly asking about intake dates; student expressly stating readiness to proceed; student proactively reinitiated contact after considering options

### 4 - Strong Opportunity
- **Definition**: A high-value opportunity for enrollment closing with substantial impact on enrollment likelihood
- **Student Signals**: Student has shown clear interest and implied readiness through questions or statements
- **Call Timing**: Occurs well after discovery phase with value clearly established
- **Context**: Major objections have been addressed, though minor concerns may remain
- **Missed Impact**: Failure to capitalize would moderately reduce enrollment likelihood
- **Examples**: Student showing positive response to program information; student asking about application processes; student expressing alignment between program and goals

### 3 - Moderate Opportunity
- **Definition**: A viable opportunity for enrollment closing with potential impact on enrollment likelihood
- **Student Signals**: Student shows interest but may have ambiguous commitment signals
- **Call Timing**: Occurs after basic discovery with some value established
- **Context**: Some objections addressed but others may need attention
- **Missed Impact**: Failure to capitalize would somewhat reduce enrollment chances
- **Examples**: Student showing general interest; student asking exploratory questions about process; student indicating potential future action

### 2 - Limited Opportunity
- **Definition**: A minimal opportunity for enrollment closing with limited impact on enrollment likelihood
- **Student Signals**: Student shows initial interest but with significant hesitation
- **Call Timing**: May occur early in consultation with limited value-building
- **Context**: Several objections remain unaddressed or program fit is uncertain
- **Missed Impact**: Failure to capitalize has minimal impact on enrollment outlook
- **Examples**: Student in early exploration phase; student with multiple lingering concerns; student giving mixed signals about readiness

### 1 - Minimal Opportunity
- **Definition**: A technically valid but extremely weak opportunity for enrollment closing
- **Student Signals**: Student shows only passing interest with clear hesitation
- **Call Timing**: Typically occurs early in consultation with minimal value-building
- **Context**: Major aspects of program fit or value remain unestablished
- **Missed Impact**: Failure to capitalize has negligible impact on enrollment likelihood
- **Examples**: Student still asking basic information questions; student expressing significant uncertainty; student giving clear signals of early research phase

## Critical Guidance: Identification vs. Scoring

**IMPORTANT**: Only closing opportunities that fit within one of the 5 types (Teaching Period Confirmation, Clear Action Plan Articulation, Documentation Submission Process Clarity, Mutual Commitment Establishment, Proactive Follow-Up Scheduling) should be identified - even when they are not ideal Score 5 opportunities.

All valid opportunities should be identified and scored according to this rubric, regardless of:
- How effectively the consultant actually responded
- Whether the opportunity seems "weak" or "strong"
- The consultant's personal closing style or approach

Opportunities scored 1-4 are still legitimate closing opportunities if they fit one of the 5 types. For example:
- A Teaching Period Confirmation opportunity where the student shows mild interest in program timing might score 2-3
- A Documentation Submission Process opportunity where program fit is still being established might score 2
- A Proactive Follow-Up Scheduling opportunity early in the consultation might score 1-2

Out-of-scope scenarios like value proposition discussions, work-study balance concerns, waitlist situations, or cross-faculty study contexts should NOT be identified as enrollment closing opportunities regardless of scoring potential.

## Verification Checklist

Before finalizing the identification and scoring of any enrollment closing opportunity, verify:

2. **Call Stage**: Has the call progressed beyond initial discovery (5-7 minutes)?
3. **Student Signals**: Has the student demonstrated some level of interest or readiness?
4. **Contextual Appropriateness**: Is this a standard enrollment scenario (not cross-faculty or waitlist)?
5. **Scope Adherence**: Does this focus on enrollment closing rather than objection handling or other patterns?
6. **Analysis Quality**: Does your opportunity analysis follow the gold standard example in structure, evidence, and depth?

If the answer to any of these questions is "no," the opportunity should not be identified or the analysis needs revision. If all are "yes," identify the opportunity and assign the appropriate score using the 1-5 rubric above.

# TAXONOMY ARTIFACT 11: DECISION-MAKING PROCESS & COMMITMENT INDICATORS
This taxonomy examines student decision-making patterns and commitment signals during sales calls that may predict enrollment follow-through and program persistence.

## 11.A: Decision Process Quality & Ownership

### 11.A.i: Autonomous Decision-Making Evidence
**Description:** Student demonstrates they are making the educational decision independently rather than due to external pressure, suggesting stronger intrinsic commitment.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for language indicating personal choice, self-directed decision-making, or ownership of the decision process rather than external mandates.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I've decided this is the right time in my life to pursue further education"
- Student: "This has been my goal for a while, and I finally feel ready to commit to it"
- Student: "Nobody's pushing me to do this - it's something I really want for myself"

### 11.A.ii: Information-Based Decision Framework
**Description:** Student uses systematic information gathering and analysis to make their educational decision rather than impulse or emotion alone.

**Identification Guidance:** Note evidence of research, comparison processes, criteria-based evaluation, or systematic consideration of factors.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I've created a spreadsheet comparing different programs based on cost, duration, and career outcomes"
- Student: "I've set specific criteria for my decision: program reputation, online format, and practical application"
- Student: "I've researched employment statistics and graduate satisfaction rates"

### 11.A.iii: Realistic Timeline & Expectation Setting
**Description:** Student demonstrates realistic understanding of the commitment involved and sets appropriate timeline expectations for their decision and program completion.

**Identification Guidance:** Look for accurate understanding of program duration, realistic planning for decision timelines, and appropriate expectations about effort required.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I understand this is a significant commitment and I'm not rushing the decision"
- Student: "I want to start in the second intake to give myself time to prepare properly"
- Student: "I know this will be challenging but I'm ready for the commitment it requires"

## 11.B: Commitment Signals & Investment Indicators

### 11.B.i: Concrete Action Planning
**Description:** Student describes specific, immediate actions they will take to move forward with enrollment, indicating genuine intention to proceed.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for specific, actionable plans with timelines rather than vague intentions to "think about it" or "get back to you."

**Examples:**
- Student: "I'll submit my application tonight and gather my documents this weekend"
- Student: "I need to check one thing with my employer, but I plan to enroll by Friday"
- Student: "I'm going to review the course outline tonight and call you back tomorrow with any questions"

### 11.B.ii: Investment Readiness Demonstration
**Description:** Student shows concrete evidence of financial and time investment readiness, suggesting serious commitment rather than casual interest.

**Identification Guidance:** Note specific mentions of financial preparation, time allocation planning, or other concrete investments made in preparation for education.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I've already set aside the money for this program"
- Student: "I've cleared my schedule and reduced my volunteer commitments to make time for study"
- Student: "I've been preparing for this decision for months and I'm ready to invest in myself"

### 11.B.iii: Stakeholder Communication & Alignment
**Description:** Student has proactively communicated with relevant stakeholders and secured their support or understanding for the educational commitment.

**Identification Guidance:** Look for evidence of family discussions, employer conversations, or other stakeholder alignment that suggests serious planning.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I've already discussed this with my partner and we're both committed to making it work"
- Student: "My manager knows I'm considering this and has offered to adjust my workload during exam periods"
- Student: "I've talked to my family about the time commitment and they're fully supportive"

## 11.C: Follow-Through & Persistence Indicators

### 11.C.i: Multi-Touch Engagement Consistency
**Description:** Student maintains consistent engagement and interest across multiple interactions, showing sustained rather than fluctuating commitment.

**Identification Guidance:** Note consistency in enthusiasm, follow-through on commitments made in previous calls, and building upon previous conversations.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I've done the research you suggested and I'm even more convinced this is the right program"
- Student: "I followed up on those alumni contacts you mentioned and had great conversations"
- Student: "Since we last spoke, I've been thinking about how this fits with my five-year plan"

### 11.C.ii: Obstacle Resolution Approach
**Description:** Student approaches potential barriers with problem-solving orientation rather than using them as reasons to withdraw from consideration.

**Identification Guidance:** Listen for active problem-solving language when challenges are discussed, seeking solutions rather than abandoning the goal.

**Examples:**
- Student: "The timing is tight, but I think I can make it work by adjusting my work schedule"
- Student: "The cost is significant, but I've looked into payment options and employer support"
- Student: "I'm concerned about the workload, but I'd rather start with fewer units than not start at all"

### 11.C.iii: Future Implementation Visualization
**Description:** Student demonstrates ability to visualize themselves as a successful student and graduate, indicating psychological commitment to the journey.

**Identification Guidance:** Note specific references to their future as a student, graduation plans, or application of learning in concrete future scenarios.

**Examples:**
- Student: "I'm already thinking about which units I'll find most challenging and planning how to manage those"
- Student: "I can see myself using these skills in my current role within the first year of the program"
- Student: "I'm excited about the graduation ceremony and having my family there to celebrate"
